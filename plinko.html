How the math works. Each chip falls through 12 rows of pegs, and at every peg it goes left or right with an even chance. So the landing slot follows the binomial distribution: the middle is by far the most common and pays little, while the outer slots are rare and pay big (29x at the very edge, which lands about 1 time in 2048). Multiplying each slot's chance by its multiplier gives the return, computed live below at 99.4%. The whole game is one clean probability curve, shaped by how you set the multipliers.
