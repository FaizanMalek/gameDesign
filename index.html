CINDERFALL is a Soulslike themed slot built around a single design idea: pacing through volatility. Long, tense stretches broken by a decisive Boss Bonus, the same rhythm as the games that inspired it. Ash, embers, a fallen king, and a bonfire that holds back the dark.

I designed the symbol set and paytable, then built the return-to-player math two ways. First a closed form model in Excel, then an independent simulation of one million spins through the real game engine. The two agree to within half a percent at 94.2% RTP, with the return deliberately concentrated into the rare bonus to fit the theme. The prototype plays in the browser and includes a live tool that re-runs the simulation on demand.

About

I hold a Bachelor of Engineering in Electronics from The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda and am completing an IT Software Development diploma at NBCC in Moncton. My foundation is mathematics: probability, statistics, and expected value from university coursework, and two consecutive first place finishes at the state level Mathematics Olympiad.

That math background is the reason a slot paytable feels natural to me. Return to player is an expected value, volatility is a standard deviation, and tuning a game to a target is a control problem. I have also spent years inside systems heavy games, including national level competitive play, which is where my sense for pacing, balance, and reward came from.

Alongside design I build. I currently lead development of a production platform during a work placement, and I shipped a live tool used by dozens of community organizations that won first place at a hackathon. CINDERFALL brings both halves together: a designed experience, backed by math I can defend, in a build that actually runs.
